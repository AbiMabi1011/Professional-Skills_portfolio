Understanding job interview preparation and performance with hands-on mock interview experience

Interview Skills

1. Lesson Overview

This lesson focused on job interview preparation and performance, emphasizing the importance of verbal and non-verbal communication, active listening, and confident presentation. It also included a mock interview activity where students acted as interviewers and interviewees, gaining firsthand experience of real-world hiring scenarios.

The objective was to develop practical interview skills, understand employer expectations, and build self-confidence for future job applications.

2. Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

  • Understand the purpose of a job interview in the hiring process.
  • Apply effective verbal and non-verbal communication techniques.
  • Prepare answers that demonstrate skills, achievements, and personality.
  • Practice mock interview sessions to identify personal strengths and weaknesses.
  • Reflect on your own performance and plan ways to improve.

3. What Is a Job Interview?

A job interview is a formal conversation between a job applicant and a company representative. Its goal is to exchange information — the interviewer evaluates whether the candidate fits the job, while the candidate learns whether the company suits their career goals.

In short: It's not just an evaluation; it's a two-way communication process.

4. The Role of Effective Communication

Interview success depends heavily on communication skills. How you speak, listen, and behave gives employers evidence of your confidence, personality, and professionalism.

Types of Communication

Verbal Communication: Clarity, tone, confidence, vocabulary.

Non-Verbal Communication: Body language, facial expressions, posture, eye contact.

Listening: Paying attention, understanding, and responding appropriately.

According to research cited in the lesson, 93% of communication impact in interviews comes from non-verbal cues (tone 38%, body language 55%).

5. The 6 C's of Effective Communication

Principle Description
Clear Present information simply and directly.
Concise Avoid unnecessary words; stay to the point.
Correct Provide accurate, truthful details.
Complete Include all relevant information.
Concrete Use examples to support your answers.
Courteous & Confident Be polite, respectful, and show self-assurance.

6. Before the Interview – Preparation Steps

Research the Company – Know their values, services, and mission.

Understand the Role – Study the job description carefully.

Prepare Your CV and Documents – Review them thoroughly.

Plan Answers for Common Questions – ("Tell me about yourself," "Why should we hire you?").

Prepare Questions to Ask the Employer – Shows interest and initiative.

Dress Professionally – Follow the company's formal or semi-formal dress code.

7. During the Interview – Key Behavioral Tips

Do's Don'ts
Maintain eye contact and smile. Avoid looking down or crossing arms.
Sit straight and show confidence. Don't slouch or fidget.
Listen carefully and respond clearly. Don't interrupt the interviewer.
Give specific examples to support answers. Don't speak in general terms.
Ask relevant questions about the job. Don't say you have no questions.

8. Structure of a Good Interview Answer

Every answer should include:

Situation / Challenge – Describe the context.

Task / Goal – What was expected of you.

Action – What you did to solve it.

Result – What was achieved or learned.

This is often called the STAR Method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

9. Active Listening and Response

Active listening helps build rapport and ensures accurate responses.

Tips:

Pay full attention.
Show that you're listening through nods or small affirmations.
Ask for clarification when unsure.
Avoid interrupting the interviewer.

10. Common Interview Questions and Examples

Question Example of a Good Answer
"Tell us about yourself." Brief background + key skills + career goal.
"What are your strengths?" "I'm good at team collaboration and meeting deadlines."
"Describe a challenge you faced." Explain how you handled a work or study-related problem using the STAR method.
"Why do you want this job?" Show alignment between your skills and company goals.
"Where do you see yourself in five years?" Express ambition and commitment to growth.

11. Mock Interview Activity (Lesson Application)

Students conducted mock interviews using their own CVs. Each participant acted as both interviewer and interviewee, and peer evaluation was done using a standardized rubric assessing:

Verbal Skills (clarity, tone, fluency)

Non-Verbal Skills (posture, eye contact, smile, confidence)

Listening & Response

Attitude & Professionalism

This hands-on experience simulated a real interview environment and provided valuable feedback for improvement.

12. Key Takeaways

Interviews test not only your qualifications but also your communication, confidence, and attitude.

Non-verbal cues play a major role in how you are perceived.

Research and preparation lead to better performance.

Mock interviews help you identify areas to improve before facing real ones.

13. My Mock Interview Experience & Learning Reflection

During our mock interview session, I had the opportunity to experience a real-world interview setup. At first, I felt slightly nervous, but I gradually became more comfortable as I focused on my preparation and communication skills.

My Strengths

Maintained good eye contact and professional posture.

Spoke clearly and confidently about my academic background and skills.

Demonstrated enthusiasm and a positive attitude.

Areas for Improvement

I need to manage my nervousness during initial minutes of the interview.

I plan to practice answering unexpected questions to be more spontaneous.

I should refine my closing statements to leave a strong final impression.

What I Learned

Preparation and practice build confidence.

Body language and tone communicate as much as words do.

Constructive feedback from peers helped me recognize my strengths and weaknesses.

Overall, this activity improved my self-awareness and taught me how to present my skills professionally under pressure.

14. My Personal Statement

The mock interview experience was a turning point in developing my professional confidence. I realized that every interview is an opportunity to show not only my qualifications but also my personality, attitude, and growth mindset.

Going forward, I aim to continuously improve my communication and interview skills through regular practice and feedback. I will focus on refining my answers, maintaining a calm tone, and showing genuine enthusiasm for the roles I apply for.

This lesson taught me to approach interviews not as a test, but as a conversation to show my potential and professional value.
